How to Start a Business in Lexington

If you plan to do business in Lexington, the first step is to register for a business permit, also known as an Occupational License. Completing the following steps will help you meet local, state, and federal requirements.

To Register Your Business with the City:

  1. Check Your Business Name and Register with the State
  2. Get a Federal Employer Identification Number (EIN)
  3. Get a Certificate of Occupancy for Your Business Location
  4. Submit Your Business Permit Application and $100 Fee
  5. Pay Taxes and Learn About Regulated Businesses

Help is available

If at any point you have questions, please email quickstart@lexingtonky.gov or call 859-258-3340.

2. Register for a Federal Employer Identification Number (EIN)

You can apply online for a federal EIN (also known as a federal Taxpayer Identification Number) for your business. The process is free and takes about 15 minutes.

Once you have an EIN, you will include it on your city Occupational License application.

Apply for a federal EIN

3. Certificate of Occupancy

A Certificate of Occupancy is a legal document that indicates that you can use your building or space legally.

Some locations already have a Certificate of Occupancy on file with the city. If so, you will need to get a copy of the existing certificate. Then you will submit it with your Occupational License application.

Find out how to get a Certificate of Occupancy

4. Apply for a Business Permit (Occupational License)

Once you have registered your business name with the state, have a federal EIN, and a Certificate of Occupancy, you are ready to apply for an Occupational License.

5. Paying Taxes and Registering Regulated Businesses

Regulated Businesses

If your business is on the list of regulated businesses, please contact the Division of Revenue for information regarding additional licensing and fees.

Filing Occupational Taxes During the Year

Once your business is licensed with the city, you will need to pay occupational tax. If your business makes:

  • less than $53,333: you pay every four months (quarterly)
  • more than $53,333: you pay every month

In addition, every business will submit several forms to the city with your end of year filings.
