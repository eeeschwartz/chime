Certificate of Occupancy for Business Location

A Certificate of Occupancy is a legal document that indicates that you can use your building or space legally. It makes sure that your space meets safety and other requirements.

Registering a Business

You will need a Certificate of Occupancy before registering for a business license with the City.

To get a Certificate of Occupancy:

1. Get a Zoning Compliance Permit

Visit the Division of Planning and ask for the zoning compliance questionnaire.

2. Apply for Certificate of Occupancy

Home-based businesses

Home-based businesses that do not bring customers/clients to the home are simple to register. Mail or bring the following to the Division or Building Inspection for an immediate Certificate of Occupancy:

  • your zoning compliance permit
  • $10 fee

Businesses with construction

Businesses in commercial zones who are performing construction will have different requirements to meet before applying for a Certificate of Occupancy. Please call 859-258-3770 to sign-up for a plan review session with The Division of Building Inspection. They take place each Tuesday morning.

Businesses with no construction

If your business is in a commercial location with no construction, you will need:
